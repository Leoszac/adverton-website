1. Parties

This Service Agreement (this “Agreement”) is between MDS LLC, a Delaware limited liability company doing business as Adverton (“Adverton,” “we,” “us,” or “our”), and the business identified in the Stripe Checkout session that accepts this Agreement (the “Client,” “you”).

At checkout, Client must provide its full legal business name, entity type, state of formation, and the full name and title of the individual accepting this Agreement. By accepting, that individual represents and warrants that they are authorized to bind the Client to this Agreement, including its multi-year commitment, reversion, indemnity, arbitration, and class-action-waiver provisions. Adverton may rely on that representation. The Client further represents that it is entering this Agreement for business and commercial purposes, not as a consumer, and that the Services are procured for its trade or business.

2. Services

Adverton will deliver the following bundled growth-management services (the “Services”), at all times as the Client's authorized agent and service provider acting on the Client's behalf and at the Client's direction (see §2.5).

2.1 Website (built, licensed during the term, transfers on completion)

  • A custom-built marketing website of up to five (5) pages, mobile-responsive, with online booking capability.
  • Migration of an existing website (if applicable) using commercially reasonable efforts and current SEO best practices designed to preserve the Client's Google search ranking. Adverton does not guarantee any specific ranking outcome (see §11).
  • Domain registration (one) and configuration, business hosting, SSL, and one (1) business email mailbox.

2.2 Recurring Services (monthly)

  • Google Business Profile (GBP) setup, optimization, maintenance, and responding to reviews on the Client's behalf with Client's approval/ratification (see §2.5).
  • Automated review requests: after a job is marked complete, a message is sent to the Client's customer requesting a review, via the Client's dedicated business number. These review-request messages are sent only to the Client's own customers and only on the consent basis the Client warrants under §2A. The review workflow is non-gating: it does not screen for or solicit only positive reviews, and offers no incentive for a review.
  • Missed-call text-back and website-lead capture: a dedicated business number automatically sends a reply text to a customer whose call to the Client is missed, and sends lead alerts to the Client/staff. Adverton honors STOP/HELP/START and maintains A2P 10DLC registration for the messaging program (see §2A).
  • Google Local Services Ads (LSA) account setup, campaign management, and ongoing optimization (media spend governed by §7).
  • Tradio CRM access (online booking, mobile CRM, scheduling, GPS routing, quotes, invoices, payments, appointment reminders), provided through a third-party partner (see §7.2).
  • One (1) monthly performance report and one (1) monthly strategy call with a dedicated account manager.

2.3 Optional Add-Ons

Additional Services may be selected at checkout or added during the term at the prices then published at adverton.net. The current catalog includes: AI Voice Receptionist, Meta Ads management, Local SEO Pro, Social Media Posts, Yelp setup & management, content updates, call tracking, Lead Marketplace Management, multi-location, extra business email, and ad-spend management. Add-on pricing is locked at the price in effect when the add-on is first activated, for the remainder of the then-current term, and may change only at renewal on the notice in §3.

2.4 Service delivery and third-party dependencies

Certain Services depend on third-party platform approvals (e.g., A2P 10DLC carrier approval for SMS features, Google verification for LSA). Where a Service cannot be delivered because of a pending or denied third-party approval outside Adverton's reasonable control, §16 (Force Majeure) applies and Adverton will activate the affected Service promptly once approval clears.

2.5 Agency, authorized content, and ratification

Where Adverton creates, posts, sends, or responds to content in the Client's name — including GBP posts, review responses, social content, and SMS to the Client's customers — Adverton acts solely as the Client's authorized agent, on the Client's account and at the Client's direction. The Client is the principal and the originating party for such communications. Client grants Adverton authority to act on its behalf for these purposes and ratifies content published within the scope of the Services. The Client remains responsible for the lawfulness of Client-supplied content, trademarks, customer lists, and the Client's underlying customer relationships. Adverton will follow the Client's reasonable instructions and the platforms' terms; Adverton is responsible for its own willful misconduct or gross negligence in performing these tasks.

2A. SMS / Text-Messaging Compliance and Consumer Consent

2A.1 Roles

The messaging program texts the Client's own customers from a dedicated business number. For these messages, the Client is the sender and the party with the relationship to the recipient; Adverton is the technology provider and the Client's agent transmitting messages on the Client's behalf. Adverton maintains the A2P 10DLC brand/campaign registration and STOP/HELP/START handling for the program.

2A.2 Message classification

  • Transactional/informational — the missed-call text-back (sent only after the customer first calls the Client) and lead alerts to the Client's own staff. These are responses to customer-initiated contact.
  • Solicited service messages — the post-job review request and reminder, and two-way relay, sent only to a customer who provided their number to the Client for service communications and has not opted out.
  • Adverton does not send untargeted marketing/promotional blasts under the base Service.

2A.3 Client consent warranty

The Client represents, warrants, and covenants that, before any phone number is submitted to or texted through the Services, the Client has obtained and will maintain all consents and authorizations required by applicable law for the messages contemplated — including, where required, prior express consent (and, for any message that is marketing or promotional in nature, prior express written consent) under the Telephone Consumer Protection Act (47 U.S.C. § 227), the Telemarketing Sales Rule, CAN-SPAM, and all applicable federal and state Do-Not-Call and telemarketing laws. The Client further warrants that each number it submits belongs to its own current customer who provided it for service communications, and that the Client has not imported purchased, rented, or third-party lists. Use of the Services does not guarantee the Client's compliance with any law, and Adverton expressly disclaims liability for the Client's non-compliance.

2A.4 Opt-out integrity

Opt-outs (STOP) are honored on a per-sender/per-Client basis; a customer's opt-out from one Client does not opt them into messages from any other Client. Adverton will not re-enable messaging to a number that has opted out except on a valid START from that number to that Client.

2A.5 Client SMS indemnity

The Client's indemnity in §13 expressly covers all claims arising from the messaging program, including TCPA/TSR claims, the Client's failure to obtain required consent, and the numbers/lists the Client provides.

2A.6 Point-of-collection opt-in

Where Adverton provides the Client's intake or booking forms, those forms will include a clear SMS opt-in disclosure at the point the customer's number is collected (e.g., “We may text you about your service. Msg & data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for help.”), and Adverton will retain the consent record per number. Where the Client collects numbers through its own channels, the Client warrants under §2A.3 that it captured equivalent consent.

5. The Website — License During Term, Transfer on Completion

5.1 Ownership and license during the term

The website Adverton builds — including its source code, design files, content-management-system access, hosting configuration, and the hosting account — is and remains Adverton's property during the term. Adverton grants the Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the website for the Client's business while the subscription is active and in good standing. During the term the Client does not acquire title, equity, or a security interest in the website; the Client subscribes to its use as part of the Services.

5.2 Subscription, not purchase

Because the website is licensed and not sold during the term, the Client owes no purchase price and no installment balance. The $799 monthly fee buys the Services and the license, not a transfer of ownership. The reference value of the website for transfer, insurance, and Schedule A purposes is $9,588; this is a fair-market reference value, not a debt or financed balance owed by the Client.

5.3 Transfer of ownership upon completion

Upon the Client's completion of the 12-month term in good standing (all monthly fees paid), Adverton will, within fourteen (14) days and at no additional charge, as a completion benefit, transfer ownership of the website to the Client by delivering: (a) an export of the website code and content in a portable archive (.zip), (b) administrative ownership of the domain name, and (c) a portable export of the Client's customer records held in Tradio (CSV), and will grant the perpetual, non-exclusive license described in §9.

5.4 Early ownership transfer (optional)

The Client may elect early transfer at any time by paying a one-time early-transfer fee equal to the remaining committed monthly fees for the term (i.e., paying out the 12-month commitment). Upon clearance, ownership transfers as in §5.3. This is an optional convenience, not an obligation, and carries no premium or penalty.

5.5 Default and early termination

(a) Notice and cure. If the Client fails to pay or otherwise materially breaches, Adverton will give written notice specifying the breach and a thirty (30)-day cure period. Adverton will not suspend the website or reclaim the domain during the cure period, and will give the Client an opportunity to export its site and customer data (§5.3/§8) before any suspension.

(b) License ends; Adverton retains its own property. If the breach is not cured, the Client's license under §5.1 terminates and Adverton may take the website offline and retain it. Because the Client never held title, this is termination of a license to Adverton's own property — not a repossession or forfeiture of anything the Client owned, and involves no double recovery.

(c) Early-termination fee — compensatory, declining pre-estimate. As liquidated damages for early termination, the Client owes a one-time early-termination fee equal to the lesser of (i) the remaining committed monthly fees for the unexpired term, or (ii) the unrecovered build value of the website, defined as $9,588 (the website's fair value) minus the total monthly fees already paid by the Client — an amount that declines toward $0 as the term progresses. The parties acknowledge and agree that, as of the date of signing, Adverton's actual damages from early termination are difficult to ascertain and are front-loaded: Adverton designs and builds the full website at the outset (a one-time cost it recovers only gradually across the 12 monthly fees), plus onboarding, third-party provisioning, and reserved delivery capacity. The early-termination fee is a reasonable, compensatory pre-estimate of that unrecovered cost — recovering value already delivered — and not a penalty. Amounts already paid for Services rendered are not refunded; the Client receives credit so that Adverton is made whole but not more than whole.

(d) No retroactive clawback. Amounts already paid for Services already rendered are not refundable and are not retroactively repriced.

5.7 Dedicated business phone number(s) — assignment and port-out

(a) Provisioning and control during the term. The dedicated business number(s) used for missed-call text-back, lead alerts, and review messages are provisioned and maintained by Adverton through its telephony provider (Twilio) during the term and assigned for the Client's use. Like the website (§5.1), a number Adverton newly provisions is controlled by Adverton during the term; the Client is licensed to use it as part of the Services.

(b) Client-supplied / ported-in numbers always return to the Client. If the Client provided or ported in an existing business number, that number remains the Client's property and Adverton will port it back to, or release it to, the Client (or the Client's designated carrier) promptly on termination, at no charge. Adverton will never retain a number the Client brought.

(c) Newly provisioned numbers — Client's right to port out. For a number Adverton newly provisioned, upon completion or termination the Client may, within thirty (30) days, request to port the number to a carrier or account of its choice. Adverton will reasonably cooperate with the standard Local Number Portability (LNP) process and provide the information the receiving carrier requires. The Client is responsible for establishing a receiving account and for all port-out, LNP, and receiving-carrier fees, plus any hourly assistance fee under §5.8.

(d) Release after the window / notice before takedown. Adverton will give written notice before releasing a number that is published on the Client's GBP or website, and an opportunity to port it, so the Client's inbound calls are not interrupted. If the Client does not request port-out within the §5.7(c) window (or, for a defaulting Client, within the §5.5(a) notice/cure period), Adverton may release, reassign, or recycle the number and makes no warranty that the number remains reachable thereafter.

(e) A2P / messaging registration. On port-out, A2P 10DLC re-registration for the number is handled by the Client's receiving carrier; Adverton's messaging-program responsibility for that number ends on transfer.

5.8 Transfer and migration costs; hourly assistance fee

(a) Client bears all transfer costs. Any third-party or out-of-pocket cost associated with transferring, exporting, porting, or migrating the website, domain, phone number(s), email, or customer data to the Client or its designated providers — including domain transfer and registration fees, number port-out / LNP fees, and any receiving-carrier or registrar charges — is the Client's responsibility in every case, regardless of the reason for the transfer and regardless of whether the Client is in good standing or in default. Adverton does not absorb these costs.

(b) Hourly assistance fee. Adverton's time spent performing or assisting with any transfer, export, port-out, or migration is billable. Adverton reserves the right to charge for this work at a rate of $150.00 per hour, billed in whole-hour increments — rounded up to the next full hour, and never billed by the minute or fraction of an hour. Adverton may provide an estimate and may require payment of this fee before releasing or completing the transfer.

(c) Relationship to the completion benefit. The standard ownership-transfer deliverables in §5.3 (code export, domain admin handover, and the one-time customer-data CSV) remain included at no hourly charge for a Client that completes the 12-month term in good standing; the §5.8(a) third-party costs and the §5.8(b) hourly fee apply to any transfer beyond those standard deliverables — including early transfers, default-driven exits, repeated or expedited requests, and migrations to third-party providers.
