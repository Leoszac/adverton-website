Local context

Why Houston electrical marketing is different.

Houston runs on its air conditioning, so the electrical work that pays here follows the heat and the storms. Gulf Coast hurricane season and the grid scares since the 2021 freeze keep standby-generator and transfer-switch calls steady, while the region's long, humid cooling season pushes panel and service upgrades to support bigger HVAC loads, EV chargers, and pool equipment.

A lot of the housing stock is sprawling postwar and newer suburban builds across a flat, fast-growing metro, so the bread-and-butter is service-panel swaps, surge protection, code-correction on remodels, and new-construction rough-ins. Texas licenses electricians at the state level through TDLR, and most jobs pull permits through the City of Houston or the surrounding county — buyers expect a licensed, insured shop, and your marketing has to say so plainly.

Why do I need electrical-specific marketing in Houston?
Houston is one of the most crowded electrical markets in the country — you're not just up against neighborhood shops but national franchises and the big AC companies that also do electrical, all bidding for the same searches. That drives up the cost per call and means a generic site and unmanaged ads get buried fast. Lead quality also swings with the seasons here: a quiet stretch can flip overnight when a freeze or a hurricane sends panel, generator, and rewire calls spiking all at once, and the shops set up to catch that demand win the year.
